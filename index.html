The Project

In this project, we collect and document best practices for extending INSPIRE models. This documentation shows how organisations can align their processes with the INSPIRE reference infrastructure. The documentation is centered on a extension methodology that describes the process, people and tools needed to make an INSPIRE extension project successful, and provides common extension approaches and explains their impact on implementation.

Site Overview

The study has three core elements: The initial survey and inventory of existing INSPIRE Model Extensions, the extension pattern catalogue and an end-to-end tutorial project. The sections will go online one by one over the next months:

  1. Introduction
  2. Results of the Survey
  3. Model Extensions Inventory
  4. The INSPIRE Model-Driven Approach
  5. The Extension Methodology
  6. The Pattern Catalogue
  7. An End-to-End Tutorial Project
  8. Conclusions and Outlook

Authors and Contributors

This study is executed and documented by wetransform and has been commissioned by Geonovum. The JRC, the European Environmental Agency and DG Environment fully support this initiative and take part in our advisory board.

Contact and Involvement

Interested in learning more or in getting involved? Send us a note to be registered as an observer or to take part in the advisory board. Or, meet us in person: We will present the study and its outcomes at the INSPIRE conference 2016.

You have an INSPIRE model extension you'd like to see included in the inventory? Fork the repository on GitHub, add your page for the model, and send us a pull request. Let us know if you are not familiar with this approach and we'll help you to get it included.
